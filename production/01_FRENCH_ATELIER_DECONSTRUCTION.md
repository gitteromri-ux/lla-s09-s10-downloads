## 1 · The Skeleton (6 acts, fixed order — this is the conversion machine)
| Act | Time (80–90s film) | Job | Emotional key |
|---|---|---|---|
| I · The World | 0–11% | Cold open on the WORLD, never the face | Intrigue / scale |
| II · Reveal | 11–27% | First face. The person behind the promise | Trust |
| III · Method | 27–47% | The teachable framework (2 factors / 6 pillars) | Clarity |
| IV · Proof | 47–64% | One number, plainly cited | Belief |
| V · Offer | 64–83% | What you actually get, concretely | Desire |
| VI · Close | 83–100% | Calm certainty + sign-off + end card | Action |

## 2 · The Cut Grammar (what makes it feel expensive)
- **WIDE↔TIGHT alternation on the clause.** Every clause = a cut. 35–45 cuts per 80s (~1 cut / 2s). Never cut mid-word; the edit *breathes with the sentence*.
- **Cold open owns different color science** than the reveal — cool/clinical vs warm Portra. The temperature snap IS the reveal.
- **B-roll carries every claim:** no sentence about the world without seeing the world. Talking head alone never exceeds ~6s.
- **Silence discipline** (Shorts variant): room tone as aesthetic. TV/promo variant (this project): score per `production/music/MUSIC_KIT.md`, ducked −12 dB under voice, hard silence 1–1.5s before the offer.

## 3 · The Graphic-Card System (the lead-gen weapon)
Three CTA moments minimum, placed ~25% / ~55% / ~85%:
1. **Identity card** (~25%) — who this is and why to keep watching. Transformation-framed.
2. **Method card** (~55%) — the teachable thing, named. Makes the offer feel learnable.
3. **Offer card** (~85%) — price/terms/urgency, plain type, zero decoration beyond brand.
Rules: ≤8 words · Fraunces display on navy `#0E1F48` · bone `#F5F2EA` type · full-frame takeover (never a bug/overlay) · card text swaps exactly on a cut · one idea per card.
**Rendered and ready:** `production/cards/png/` (vertical + horizontal, all 5 cards).

## 4 · Typography & Brand Rails
Fraunces (display) · Inter SemiBold (captions 48–52px, Y 60–65%, swap on cut) · lockup only on navy, never cropped, always "by eTeacher Group" · banned: Arial/Helvetica/system fonts, banned verbs (unlock/supercharge/hack/10x/biohack), no exclamation marks in VO, no emoji, gain-frame only (no fear-of-death).

## 5 · Why it converts (the mechanics, no fluff)
- **Cold open = pattern interrupt**: feed-scrollers see a *world*, not an ad face.
- **Clause-cutting = retention**: a new visual every ~2s reads as "premium documentary," not stock montage.
- **Cards = the ad admits it's an ad, three times, cleanly** — Meta lead-gen audiences convert on explicit, confident asks, not hints.
- **One number, plainly cited** (Harvard / 120,000 / 14 years) beats ten claims.
- **Calm close** — the genre's signature: certainty sells, enthusiasm discounts.
